# Economy

## Currencies

| Currency | Source | Sink |
|---|---|---|
| **Gems** (premium) | Brawl Pass, offers, paid shop | Buy coins/credits, Brawl Pass, skins, offers |
| **Coins** | Star Drops, quests, Trophy Road, offers | Upgrade brawlers (Power Level) |
| **Power Points** | Star Drops, quests, offers | Upgrade brawlers (PP + Coins required) |
| **Credits** (Brawler Credits) | Star Drops, quests, offers | Unlock specific new brawlers |
| **Tokens** | Match play, quests | Progress the Brawl Pass |
| **Bling** | Brawl Pass, events | Buy cosmetics (skins, pins) |

The currency stack is deliberately wide — each has a distinct role. A clone that collapses them into one "universal" currency will lose the layered progression feel.

## Coins: The Bottleneck

Coins are the **tightest resource for f2p**. Power Level upgrades need both Power Points and Coins; PP flows in fast, Coins flow in slow.

Typical daily Coin income for a moderately active f2p: 100–400 Coins. Upgrading a brawler from P9 → P10 costs 1,900 Coins. Maxing a full brawler: ~5,000 Coins. Doing the roster (~75 brawlers): ~375,000 Coins.

The pacing is intentional. It gates progression to encourage either patience or monetisation.

## Credits (Brawler-Specific)

- Each brawler has a **credit track**. Accumulate ~N credits to unlock.
- Credits are specific to a brawler at launch (the "featured" new brawler), then rotate out
- **Starr Drops occasionally grant credits for any locked brawler**, helping over time
- New-brawler-exclusive challenges accelerate credit drops for that brawler

## Brawl Pass (Season Pass)

- **$5/month** (Pro track)
- **Free track** also progresses — gives tokens, some coins, 1 Pro's lesser rewards
- Pro adds a Chromatic brawler + skin + bling + bonus gems
- Tiers unlock by earning **tokens** from play + quests

### Dedicated Pass Calendars

- **Pro+ Pass**: a higher tier with guaranteed best-in-slot rewards
- **Brawl Pass Plus**: an expansion offering cosmetic-focused rewards

These are optional upsells. Not required to play or progress.

## Star Drops (Lootbox)

- On match completion: chance to receive a Star Drop
- Tiers: Common → Rare → Epic → Mythic → Legendary
- Higher-trophy brawlers increase legendary drop chance
- Contents: coins, PP, credits, gadgets, star powers, skins, pins

### Pity Timer

- Supercell has confirmed (implied via patch notes) that legendary-tier drops are **pity-capped** after N non-legendary drops
- This prevents streaks of bad luck from making the game feel unfair

### Transparency

- Star Drops show the full possible reward pool
- **Legendary drops are celebrated** with a specific animation/music

## Shop

- **Daily offers**: rotating deals, usually Brawl Pass / skin / cosmetic / gem + coin packs
- **Featured offers**: time-limited, premium (usually skins)
- **Coin / PP / Gem packs**: standard tier scaling

Shop is kept clean — not pushy, not overwhelming.

## Monetisation Posture

- **No ads** in the core game (a few opt-in video ads for minor rewards exist)
- **No real-money in-match items** (no buy-a-revive, no super-charge-for-money)
- **Skins are cosmetic + lockable sfx/animations** — never gameplay-affecting
- **Paid shortcuts are always gem-based**, never $-direct — one layer of abstraction between real money and gameplay impact

This posture has been kept for years as a competitive and ethical posture.

## Shop Purchases Gated by Currency

- **Skins**: usually 25–300 gems, or rare coins offers
- **Gadgets/Star Powers**: can be purchased with coins (bypasses Starr Drop luck)
- **Brawler unlocks**: credits + shop offers

Shop **never paywalls a brawler permanently** — every brawler is eventually reachable for f2p via credits.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Random-only brawler unlock**. Bad. Players who want a specific brawler should have a deterministic path (credits).
- **Coin-only paywalls on essential gameplay**. Star Powers and Gadgets are essential — they should be coin-or-luck, not gem-locked.
- **Energy / stamina caps.** None. Brawl Stars lets you play unlimited matches; that's the whole retention model.
- **Timed battle-pass lock-outs**. If you miss a Brawl Pass season, that chromatic brawler eventually rotates into the normal pool. Nothing is permanently paywalled.

## Power League Economy

Power League is **played on pre-maxed brawlers** — so the economy of levelling brawlers does not affect PL matches directly.

However, **owning more brawlers** = more choice during PL draft. So the brawler-collection economy still matters: a narrow roster limits draft options.

## Minimum Viable Economy

For a clone:
1. Coin (upgrade currency)
2. Gem (premium)
3. Credits / character-unlock currency (preserves deterministic unlocks)
4. Tokens (season pass)
5. Season pass monthly
6. Star Drops as the loot-box reward

Skip: mastery rewards, bling, pins — add later.

## Sinks Relative to Faucets

Design a spreadsheet:
- Per day of f2p play: ~X coins, ~Y PP, ~Z credits earned
- Per full upgrade of one brawler: cost in each
- Roster-wide costs
- Target pacing: a dedicated f2p can max their favourite brawler in ~3 weeks; full roster in ~1–2 years

A clone that prices faucets/sinks outside this pacing will feel too fast (no progression pressure) or too slow (retention collapses).
