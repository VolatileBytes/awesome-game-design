# Economy

## Currencies

| Currency | Earn | Spend |
|---|---|---|
| **Aeos Coins** | Matches (capped weekly), daily missions | Unite Licenses (Pokémon), Battle Items, Held Items |
| **Aeos Gems** | Paid only (with rare drops) | Licenses, cosmetics, passes |
| **Aeos Tickets** | Events, login bonuses | Holowear, event bundles |
| **Fashion Tickets** | Battle Pass, events | Trainer / Pokémon cosmetics |
| **Item Enhancers** | Battle Pass, events, daily tasks | Upgrade Held Items |

## Weekly Cap on Aeos Coin Earn

Important: the primary grind currency (Aeos Coins) is **capped weekly** (~2,100/week). This forces spacing — you can't binge to riches.

- Matches grant diminishing coins per day
- Prevents grinding exploits
- Encourages regular daily play (habit-forming)

## License Economy

- New Pokémon licenses: 8,000–14,000 Aeos Coins (~4–7 weeks of capped earn)
- Or 460 Aeos Gems (~$10)
- Event limited Pokémon: often "free if earned by date X" — creates retention hooks

## Cosmetic Economy

### Holowear (Pokémon skins)
- **Standard Holowear**: 600 gems (~$12)
- **Premium Holowear** (with Unite cinematic alt): 1,200 gems
- **Signature Holowear**: 2,400 gems, ultra-rare

### Trainer (player avatar) cosmetics
- Hair, clothing, accessories
- Much cheaper; primarily earn-able
- Strong personalization for login lobby

## Battle Pass

- ~$10 (~600 gems)
- ~60 tiers
- Rewards: Aeos Coins, Gems, Tickets, Holowear, Item Enhancers
- 2-month cycles

## F2P vs Paid Balance

- F2P can grind to unlock most Pokémon over ~6 months
- Competitive rank requires high-tier Held Items — F2P takes ~3 months per item to max
- Paid players skip these grinds
- **Power-gap** is small but real: a Master-tier player with Lv 30 items vs Lv 20 items has a measurable edge

## Event Economy

- Limited-time event currencies
- Event store with unique rewards
- Event tickets earn by playing during event window (FOMO-ish)

## Whale Spending Ceiling

- Full roster of Holowear: ~$200+
- Every license: ~$100 skip-grind
- Battle Pass every season: ~$60/year
- Premium whales drop $500–1000+/year on cosmetics

## Anti-Patterns

- **Pay-to-win Pokémon**: if a paid Pokémon is statistically dominant, the economy fails trust
- **Excessive event FOMO**: 5 simultaneous events all expiring = stress, not fun
- **Loot-box mechanics** on Pokémon: direct buy is cleaner; obscure drop rates on licenses are problematic
- **Over-capping Aeos Coins**: players hit the cap, stop playing for the week → engagement drop

## Daily Loop Incentive

- First 3 matches/day: bonus Aeos Coins
- Daily missions: 5 tasks, refresh at midnight
- Login bonus: Trainer Level XP + minor rewards
- 7-day login streak: bigger reward (free Pokémon license trial)

## Monetization Design Goals

- Never pay-to-win (power)
- Always pay-to-style (cosmetics)
- Grind-to-win (you can F2P unlock everything over time)
- Paid = save time, not buy wins
