# Progression Design

Two tracks: **Trainer Level** (account-wide) and **Ranked** (seasonal).

## Trainer Level

Monotonic account XP:
- Gained from every match
- Level ups unlock: Battle Items, Held Item slots, features, rewards
- Level 30 → Ranked unlock
- Plateau around mid levels; serves as onboarding gating

## Unite License

Each Pokémon is a unlock, purchased via:
- **Aeos Coins** (earned in-game, slow)
- **Aeos Gems** (premium, paid)
- Event rewards (limited-time Pokémon unlocks)

Typical cost: 8,000–10,000 Aeos Coins (~3–5 weeks of grind per Pokémon F2P).

## Held Items

- Earn the items themselves via progression
- **Upgrade** them with Item Enhancers (each Held Item has 3 tiers: Lv 1 → Lv 30)
- Max-tier items significantly outperform base — high-tier play requires high-tier items

## Ranked Ladder

Seasonal (~2 months):
- Tiers: Beginner 3 → 2 → 1 → Great → Expert → Veteran → Ultra → Master → Master Ball
- Each tier has sub-ranks
- Performance points earned per match based on match W/L + personal metrics (damage, KDA, scoring)
- **Master Ball** = elite global leaderboard
- End-of-season rewards based on highest rank

## Battle Pass (Battle Pass Season)

- ~60 tiers
- Free and Premium tracks
- Rewards: coins, gems, holowear, license shards, event tickets
- ~2 months duration per season
- Premium is ~$10

## Daily / Weekly Missions

- **Daily**: 3–5 tasks (play matches, deal damage with X Pokémon, score 30 points)
- **Weekly**: larger tasks
- Rewards: Aeos Coins, Fashion Tickets, Item Enhancers

## Events

- **Seasonal events**: new Pokémon debut, themed holowear
- **Collab events**: alt-holowear inspired by Pokémon anime, Masters, or even other franchises (limited)
- **Ranked-only events**: bonus rewards for climbing

## Onboarding

- Tutorial: 15 minutes, teaches:
  - Virtual sticks
  - Attacking wild Pokémon
  - Scoring energy
  - Basic move usage
- First ~10 matches vs bots to smooth difficulty curve
- Starter roster: ~5 beginner-friendly Pokémon unlocked free on day one

## Roster Progression

- Earning license on 5–10 Pokémon feels natural within the first few weeks
- The game nudges players to try different roles by rotating free-to-try Pokémon weekly
- Competitive play requires at least 2–3 mastered Pokémon (for draft flexibility)

## Competitive Structure

- **Master Ball** global leaderboard
- **World Championships**: official Pokémon World Championships now feature Unite
- **League Cup** and **Championship Series** for aspiring pros
- Regional qualifiers feed into majors

## Anti-Patterns

- **Held Item pay-gate**: if max-tier items are only paid → P2W accusation
- **Ranked smurfing**: new accounts stomping low ranks; MMR must detect & match appropriately
- **New-Pokémon imbalance**: releasing a new Pokémon strong = players complain about pay-to-win license; tune before release
- **Battle pass FOMO abuse**: keep it fun, not punishing to lapsed players

## Item Enhancer System

Key progression knob:
- Each Held Item has 30 levels
- Each level needs Item Enhancers (the item-upgrade currency)
- Levels 1→10: easy (free grind)
- Levels 20→30: expensive (weeks of grind or ~$20 per item)

This is where the "soft pay gate" lives. F2P can reach the top eventually; paid players get there faster.
