# Economy — Slay the Spire

Single-player; no real money. The economy is **gold-vs-HP-vs-cards**.

## Resources

| Resource | Earned | Spent |
|---|---|---|
| Gold | Combat/elite/boss drops, events, shops selling potions | Shop: cards, relics, potions, card removal |
| HP | Max HP boosts (rare), healing potions, rest nodes | Damage from combat, negative events, specific cards |
| Cards (deck) | Post-combat draft, events, shop | Card removal at shop/events |
| Relics | Elite/boss drops, events, shop, treasure nodes | Not spent; permanent |
| Potions | Combat drops (~40% chance), events, shop | Consumed (1 use) or sold for gold |
| Energy | Gained 3/turn (default) | Spent per card play |

## Gold

- Combat drop: 10–20.
- Elite drop: 25–35.
- Boss drop: 95–105.
- Event occasionally grants large sum with risk.

Gold doesn't persist run-to-run. Spend it all before reaching the boss; unspent gold is wasted.

## Shop

Each act shop offers:
- 5 cards (random rarities, some colorless)
- 2 colorless cards
- 3 relics
- 3 potions
- 1 card-removal service

Prices:
- Card: 45–165 gold depending on rarity.
- Relic: 150–300.
- Potion: 50–100.
- Card-removal: 75 (first), 125 (second), 175 (third) per act — scales up within and across acts.

## Deck Economy

The deck is a literal economy: every card is an ongoing tax on your draw quality. A 10-card deck draws your best 5 every turn; a 40-card deck draws random junk.

Principles:
- **Start thin**: starter 10 + 1–2 cards per floor.
- **Remove strikes/defends**: the 5 Strikes + 5 Defends are filler.
- **Skip mediocre drafts**: "skip" is a legal choice and often correct.
- **Don't add for free**: events that add a Curse are often worth the cost-of-removal to avoid.

## Potion Economy

- Max 3 slots (relic expands to 5).
- Selling a potion yields ~20–40 gold.
- Saving a potion for a boss vs. burning for a tough elite = real decision.
- Potion Belt (relic) +2 slots.
- Alchemize (relic, Watcher) gives a free potion each combat.

## Relic Economy

No direct cost for most relics (dropped free from elites/bosses/treasure). Cost structure is *opportunity*:
- Picking this relic = not seeing the other 2 relic options.
- Boss relic = accepting its drawback.
- Some relics penalize if taken with specific deck (e.g. Blue Candle forces you to play a curse).

## Event Economy

Events are rolls of dice on gold/HP/cards. Most common events:
- **Gold trader**: lose max HP for bonus gold.
- **Card trader**: lose a card, gain a random one of higher rarity.
- **Relic event**: pay gold or lose HP for a relic.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Pay-to-win monetization**: doesn't apply — game is one-time purchase, no IAP.
- **Grind loops**: no grind; progression is unlock + ascension.
- **Currency sinks that feel mandatory**: shops are optional.
- **Opaque drop rates**: community data is public; devs have published balance notes.

## Card Removal as the Primary Sink

The single most-used gold sink in a successful run is card removal. Removing Strikes + Defends → thinner deck → more consistent draws. Most runs spend 250–500 gold on removal across 3 acts.

## Why No IAP Works

- Price is one-time (~$25 desktop, ~$10 mobile).
- Endless depth (20 ascensions × 4 characters × infinite seed variation).
- Post-launch DLC (The Watcher added for free; Slay the Spire 2 is a sequel, not expansion).
- Community + mod ecosystem extends content for free.

The game's economy is **playing the game**, not buying progress.
