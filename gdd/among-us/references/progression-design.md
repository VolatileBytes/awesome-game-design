# Progression Design — Among Us

Among Us keeps progression light on purpose. Matches don't carry between sessions in any mechanical sense — every match is a fresh game on equal footing. Progression exists for collection, identity, and casual engagement.

## Progression Axes

1. **Account Level** — account-tied XP track.
2. **Cosmetic Collection** — hats, skins, visors, pets, nameplates.
3. **Stats** — match wins/losses by role, task completion.
4. **Beans + Stars** — two currencies (free + premium).
5. **Cosmicubes** — themed cosmetic collections.

None gate gameplay. All are bling.

## Account Level

XP from match participation:
- Win as crewmate: +XP.
- Win as impostor: +XP.
- Loss still grants reduced XP.
- Task completion bonuses.

Level cap: effectively uncapped; levels display next to name.

No unlocks via level — purely a status/engagement indicator.

## Currencies

### Beans (soft currency)

- Earned from matches (tasks completed, wins).
- Daily login bonus.
- Quest completion.
- Spend on: cosmicubes, nameplates, select cosmetics.

### Stars (hard currency)

- Purchased with real money.
- Spend on: premium cosmicubes, exclusive skin bundles, themed packs.
- Also earned sparingly from major achievements.

### Pods (legacy PC cosmetic currency)

- Older cosmetic shop currency on PC version.
- Largely replaced by Bean/Star system on newer builds.

## Cosmicubes

Themed bundles of cosmetics:
- Visual theme (e.g., Steampunk, Food, Holiday, Crossover).
- Contains: hats, skins, visors, pet, nameplate.
- Unlocked by spending Beans/Stars.
- Progressive reveal — each "pod" unlocks one cosmetic until cube complete.

Players can work toward specific cubes matching their aesthetic.

## Cosmetic Slots

Per-player customization:
- **Color**: 12 base colors + limited unlock slots.
- **Hat**: wide catalog (hundreds).
- **Skin**: alternative costume body.
- **Visor**: facial accessory.
- **Pet**: follows player around map.
- **Nameplate**: background styling on player name.

Cosmetics layer freely; no conflict or matching required.

## Daily / Weekly Quests

Added later-game:
- "Play X matches."
- "Win X as impostor."
- "Complete X tasks."

Rewards: Beans, occasional Cosmicube keys.

No FOMO — missing quests doesn't block anything.

## Achievements / Stats

Per-match stats tracked:
- Wins/losses by role.
- Tasks completed.
- Impostor kills.
- Bodies reported.
- Sabotages called/fixed.

Displayed on profile. No gameplay reward.

## Friend System

- Add friends via username / code.
- Join friend lobbies.
- Limited party features.

Primarily social; some cross-play friend-code challenges.

## Ranked Mode (2023+)

Added as separate queue:
- MMR-tracked skill rating.
- Tier system (e.g., Bronze → Legend).
- Season rewards: unique cosmetics for rank reached.

Seasonal cycle (~3 months).

Ranked is opt-in; casual remains primary mode.

## Roles Mode Unlocks

Role variants (Scientist, Engineer, Shapeshifter, Guardian Angel) available from launch. No unlock — host toggles in settings.

Some modes locked behind map purchase (Fungle, Airship free now; legacy pricing varied).

## Monetization

### Free on Mobile

- Free download with ads.
- Ad-free purchase removes ads.
- Cosmetic DLC available (Cosmicubes, themed packs).

### Paid on PC/Console

- One-time purchase (~$5 PC, more on console).
- No ads.
- Cosmetic DLC same as mobile.

No pay-to-win; no gameplay-affecting purchases.

## Design Philosophy

### Every Match Equal Ground

No player starts with mechanical advantage. Level 1 and Level 500 play identical games. Preserves fairness — critical for social deduction.

### Cosmetic Identity Only

Your hat/skin/color is your identity in lobby. Impostor wears same cosmetics as crewmate — no visual tells from appearance.

### Casual-First Cadence

No daily login required. No missed content. No seasonal wipe anxiety. Come back any time.

### Ranked as Separate Track

Competitive players have ranked lane; casual untouched. Skill-based matchmaking isolated so casual lobbies stay mixed-skill.

### Collection > Power

Progression is about expressing identity and collecting fun items. The appeal is choosing your look, not earning a better weapon.

## Rejected Patterns

- **Paid impostor chance modifier**: would break fairness.
- **XP-gated roles**: roles are host-configured, not unlock-gated.
- **Map unlocks via progression**: would split playerbase.
- **Battle-pass-style FOMO**: occasional themed passes but no critical gating.
- **Skill-based matchmaking in casual**: preserves new-player friendliness.
